How It Works

The 4-Stage Process

From raw agricultural waste to NHAI-certified bio-bitumen — a fully engineered thermo-chemical pipeline.

01
01

Raw Material & Pelletization

Agricultural waste (rice husk, sugarcane bagasse, cotton stalk) is sourced, dried, and compressed into uniform 6mm fuel pellets for consistent pyrolysis feed.

6mmPellet Size
02
02

Pyrolysis & Bio-Oil Extraction

Pellets undergo thermo-chemical conversion at 450–550°C in an oxygen-free reactor. Bio-oil (20–25% yield) is condensed and collected.

450–550°CProcess Temp
03
03

Refining & Blending

Bio-oil is refined and blended with conventional VG-30 bitumen at 15–30% ratio. The blend is homogenized and quality-tested per CSIR-CRRI specifications.

15–30%Bio-Oil Ratio
04
04

Testing & NHAI Certification

Final product undergoes penetration, softening point, ductility, and viscosity tests in NABL-accredited labs. NHAI/BIS certification obtained before market dispatch.

NABLLab Certified

What You Get

Five Revenue Streams from One Plant

Every tonne of feedstock generates multiple sellable outputs — stacking revenue and maximising plant economics.

🛢️

Bio-Oil / Pyrolysis Oil

60–70% of dry biomass weight (fast pyrolysis)

  • Industrial boiler & furnace fuel
  • Bio-bitumen feedstock for roads
⚫

Bio-Char / Charcoal

25–35% of biomass (slow pyrolysis optimised for char)

  • Soil amendment & carbon sequestration
  • Charcoal briquettes for industry & export
💨

Syngas (Producer Gas)

10–20% of biomass; 60–80% in flash pyrolysis

  • Recirculated to heat the reactor (self-sufficient)
  • Power generation via gas engines/turbines
🖤

Carbon Black (Tyre)

~330 kg per tonne of waste tyres processed

  • Rubber & tyre manufacturing reinforcement
  • Printing inks & pigments
⛽

Pyrolysis Fuel Oil (Plastic)

600–930 litres per tonne of plastic input

  • Industrial diesel substitute for boilers & generators
  • Blending with conventional diesel (up to 30%)
